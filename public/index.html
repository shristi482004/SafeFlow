The Challenge

The Chaos of Stadium Incidents

Stadiums are highly volatile environments. When emergencies strike—be it a crowd surge, medical collapse, missing child, or women's safety SOS—operators are overwhelmed with disparate telemetry, while attendees face language barriers and low-light areas.

SafeFlow resolves this complexity by orchestrating three specialized AI agents to establish situational awareness, calculate safety routing, and generate immediate, multilingual public updates.

The Pipeline

Orchestrated Agent Intelligence

Three autonomous AI Agents collaborate sequentially to process incidents and generate safety responses.

1

Observe (Agent 1)

The **Awareness Agent** analyzes live sector telemetry (crowd levels, lighting, guards) to calculate risk metrics, categories, and key observations.

2

Reason (Agent 2)

The **Decision Agent** designs a resource coordination plan (stewards, EMTs, police) and logs safety action items, subject to operator approval.

3

Act (Agent 3)

The **Communication Agent** drafts Hindi/English audio script announcements for the public PA, direct user SMS broadcasts, and ground briefings.

Mathematical Safety

Dynamic Dijkstra Routing

SafeFlow models the stadium as a weighted walkway graph ($V,E$). When pathfinding, edge weights are dynamically computed using current conditions:

- Crowd Density: Exponentially increases weight to divert people away from bottlenecks.
- Vulnerable Profiles: Adds penalty modifiers to low-light stands for solo women and elderly attendees.
- Sector Incidents: Applies near-infinite weight to blockages, rendering hazardous sectors impassable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about how SafeFlow runs? Read our technical FAQ.

How does the Multi-Agent orchestration work?
SafeFlow uses a sequential chain-of-thought orchestration. First, the Awareness Agent triages the stadium state to establish threat levels. Next, the Decision Agent evaluates resource routing. Finally, the Communication Agent formats local broadcasts.
What is the Human-in-the-Loop gate?
To ensure safety, the AI Decision Agent's resource dispatches and announcements are placed in a pending state. Operators must review, edit, or approve the action plan in their console before broadcasts execute.
How does SafeFlow auto-detect languages?
The Web Speech API transcribes spectator voice input. Our client-side analyzer runs Unicode block tests (such as script matching for Devanagari) to auto-detect Hindi/English and dynamically switches labels and TTS output.
What happens if the Gemini API is rate-limited or fails?
SafeFlow features a high-fidelity local Mock AI Engine. It mirrors the exact output schema, risk categories, and Dijkstra paths of the live Gemini models, ensuring zero downtime for observers.
